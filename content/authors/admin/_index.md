My broad research interests lie in studying inequality in access to economic opportunity along the gender, caste, and class dimensions in South Asia. 
I currently serve as India Director for Development Data Lab, where I am collaborating with Professors {{< staticref "https://samuelasher.com" "newtab" >}}Sam Asher{{< /staticref >}} and {{< staticref "https://www.paulnovosad.com" "newtab" >}}Paul Novosad{{< /staticref >}} on multiple projects including building a multi-year gender-economics research and open data agenda in South Asia, fundraising for our big, open data approach to hi-res policy and research in India and leading outreach efforts beyond the academic community. 

Some projects that I have bottomlined and brought to completion at Development Data Lab include a large-scale {{< staticref "https://www.devdatalab.org/judicial-bias" "newtab" >}}study of in-group gender and religious bias in the Indian judiciary{{< /staticref >}}, development of the largest {{< staticref "https://www.devdatalab.org/judicial-data" "newtab" >}}open-access judicial data platform{{< /staticref >}} and coordinating deployment of excess mortality data on our open access Covid data platform that lead to an {{< staticref "https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.abm5154" "newtab" >}}interdisciplinary paper published by Science{{< /staticref >}} on estimates of excess Covid mortality in India during the pandemic.

I am a June 2020 graduate of the School of Public and International Affairs at Princeton University. Prior to graduate school, I worked on large-scale evaluations of education and early childhood development programs across Madhya Pradesh and Tamil Nadu with J-PAL South Asia in India.
